Durga Puja 2025:
Day-by-Day Schedule (Starting from Day 1)

Here is a detailed schedule of the main days and significant preceding events:

Day 1 – Pratipada: 22 Sep 2025 (Monday)

This is the first day of Shardiya Navratri and is marked by Ghatasthapana or Kalash Sthapana, symbolizing the invocation of Goddess Durga’s power on earth. Devotees install a sacred pot filled with water, mango leaves, and coconut, which is worshipped for nine days. This day represents the awakening of divine feminine energy, preparing the stage for the grand Durga Puja rituals that follow.

Day 2 – Dwitiya: 23 Sep 2025 (Tuesday)

The second day is dedicated to Maa Brahmacharini, the form of Goddess Durga who embodies devotion, austerity, and penance. She carries a rosary and water pot, symbolizing knowledge and discipline. Devotees observe fasts and meditate on her blessings for strength and spiritual growth. Rituals include chanting and lighting lamps to invoke purity and courage in life.

Day 3 – Tritiya: 24 Sep 2025 (Wednesday)

On this day, devotees worship Maa Chandraghanta, the warrior goddess who symbolizes bravery and grace. She is depicted riding a tiger with a crescent moon on her forehead, symbolizing beauty and fearlessness. Worshipping her removes obstacles and negative energies. Rituals include offering flowers, fruits, and chanting mantras to gain peace and valor in life.

Day 4 – Chaturthi: 25 Sep 2025 (Thursday)

This day is devoted to Maa Kushmanda, believed to have created the universe with her divine smile. She is worshipped as the source of life and cosmic energy. Devotees light lamps and prepare offerings, believing that worshipping her brings health, wealth, and prosperity. Her blessings are sought to remove sorrow, illness, and confusion from life.

Day 5 – Panchami: 26 Sep 2025 (Friday)

The fifth day honors Maa Skandamata, the mother of Lord Kartikeya (Skanda). She is worshipped as the embodiment of motherly love and strength. Devotees believe her blessings bring wisdom, power, and salvation. Rituals include prayers with flowers and sweets, symbolizing devotion to both mother and child. This day spiritually prepares devotees for the core Durga Puja.

Day 6 – Maha Panchami: 27 Sep 2025 (Saturday)

This is the formal beginning of Durga Puja festivities. Rituals like Bilva Nimantran are performed, where Goddess Durga is invited into homes and pandals through symbolic offerings. It is believed that this day marks the descent of the Goddess on earth to destroy evil forces. Devotees decorate pandals, light lamps, and start preparing for the grand celebrations ahead.

Day 7 – Maha Shashthi: 28 Sep 2025 (Sunday)

Maha Shashthi marks the unveiling of Goddess Durga’s idol and the beginning of elaborate rituals. Ceremonies like Kalparambha, Bodhan, Amantran, and Adhivas take place. Devotees welcome Maa Durga with chants, flowers, and incense, symbolizing her arrival from Kailash to her maternal home. This day fills the air with festivity, joy, and anticipation of the sacred days to come.

Day 8 – Maha Saptami: 29 Sep 2025 (Monday)

On Maha Saptami, the Goddess is worshipped with the ritual of Nabapatrika (Kolabou Puja), where nine plants symbolizing the nine forms of Durga are bathed in the river and placed beside the idol. This symbolizes fertility, prosperity, and nature’s blessings. Devotees offer prayers with flowers, dhunuchi dance, and music, marking the goddess’s active battle against evil forces.

Day 9 – Maha Ashtami: 30 Sep 2025 (Tuesday)

Considered the most important day of Durga Puja, Maha Ashtami is dedicated to the fierce form of the Goddess. Rituals include Kumari Puja (worship of young girls as Durga) and Sandhi Puja, performed at the juncture of Ashtami and Navami, symbolizing the exact moment of Devi’s victory over Mahishasura. This day is filled with chanting, drumming, and offerings, signifying divine triumph.

Day 10 – Maha Navami: 1 Oct 2025 (Wednesday)

Maha Navami marks the culmination of Durga’s battle against evil. The rituals include Maha Arti, Balidan (symbolic sacrifice), and Navami Homa. Devotees offer prayers for protection, peace, and prosperity. The atmosphere is filled with spiritual fervor, music, and dance. This day reflects the final stage of worship before the Goddess prepares to depart from her devotees’ homes.

Day 11 – Vijaya Dashami: 2 Oct 2025 (Thursday)

Vijaya Dashami, the last day, is marked by both joy and sorrow as devotees bid farewell to Maa Durga. The idol immersion or Visarjan takes place, symbolizing her return to Kailash. Women participate in Sindoor Khela, smearing vermillion on each other, symbolizing strength and marital bliss. It marks the victory of good over evil and the promise of the Goddess’s return next year.
